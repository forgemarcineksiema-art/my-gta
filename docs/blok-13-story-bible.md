# Blok 13: Rewir Story Bible

## Canon Pitch

Blok 13: Rewir is a bitterly funny single-player micro-open-world about a returning nobody who gets dragged into a local war over parking, garages, shop access, paperwork, debt, and a developer plan dressed up as "rewitalizacja".

The game is not about saving the world. It is about saving enough of one estate to still belong somewhere.

## Genre Promise

```txt
micro-open-world story sandbox
+ episode-like missions
+ Przypal and World Memory
+ gruz driving
+ local social consequences
+ bitter comedy
```

The player should feel:

- "I know this place."
- "These idiots remember what I did."
- "This stupid errand is somehow part of a bigger conflict."
- "The controls are simple, but the world is messy."

## Protagonist

Working name: **Marek "Mlody" Laska**.

Role:

- player character;
- returns to Block 13 after a failed job outside the estate;
- starts as a broke, tired opportunist;
- slowly becomes someone the estate depends on or blames.

Personal reason to stay:

```txt
His family flat is threatened by an absurd administration debt and a developer buyout.
At first he wants money and paperwork.
By the end he has to choose what kind of rewir Block 13 becomes.
```

He is not a superhero and not a clean gangster. He is practical, sarcastic, embarrassed to care, and too used to solving problems badly.

## Core Conflict

The visible conflict is parking.

The real conflict is control:

- who owns space;
- who controls paperwork;
- who gets to stay;
- who profits from chaos;
- who gets blamed when the estate reacts.

Season 1 pressure:

```txt
Spoldzielnia says it is cleaning up the estate.
Developer says it is building premium apartments.
Residents hear one thing: garages and parking disappear.
Everyone starts using the protagonist for their own petty war.
```

## Tone

The story should be funny, but not weightless.

Good tone:

- a parking dispute sounds ridiculous, then affects missions;
- a shop receipt becomes evidence;
- a sofa contains paperwork by accident;
- a shop ban is both funny and mechanically annoying;
- an NPC line lands as a joke, then the world changes slightly.

Bad tone:

- random meme line with no consequence;
- endless profanity without a joke;
- misery without pace;
- deep lore monologues;
- parody so broad that the estate stops feeling like a place.

## Main Cast

### Mlody

Player character. Came back "for a moment". Keeps discovering that moments on Block 13 have administrative consequences.

Gameplay role:

- player movement, vehicle, interaction, mission choices.

Motivation:

- fix family flat/debt;
- make money;
- avoid becoming the estate's permanent problem;
- eventually decide whether to defend or exploit the rewir.

### Bogus "Prezes Lawki"

Local bench strategist. Treats the estate like a geopolitical board.

Gameplay role:

- early mission giver;
- teaches local geography;
- frames parking and rewir conflicts.

Obsession:

- parking as civilization.

### Halina Radar

Window neighbor, alarm system, gossip relay, moral court.

Gameplay role:

- Przypal alarm source;
- World Memory voice;
- reacts to noise, property damage, chase, and repeated chaos.

Obsession:

- knowing everything before it happens.

### Zenon Paragon

Shopkeeper. Believes receipts, cameras, debts, and bans are the pillars of society.

Gameplay role:

- shop/economy;
- ShopTrouble;
- service denial/consequence;
- mission hub for petty paperwork conflicts.

Obsession:

- proof of purchase as moral proof.

### Rysiek Rura

Garage mechanic. Can fix anything badly enough for it to move.

Gameplay role:

- vehicle tutorial;
- gruz repair/upgrades;
- garage missions;
- vehicle comedy.

Obsession:

- every car has a soul and most of them are offended.

### Lolek Archiwista

Drunk/informant who remembers the estate through gossip.

Gameplay role:

- rumor source;
- post-event commentary;
- World Memory exposition without lore dumps.

Obsession:

- "archiving" shame and truth.

### Sierzant Babel

Local officer. Wants order, but the estate constantly turns order into farce.

Gameplay role:

- patrol pressure;
- chase hints;
- Przypal escalation;
- future light police layer.

Obsession:

- making one clean report that nobody ruins.

### Prezes Kobylecki

Spoldzielnia boss. Petty bureaucrat with real power.

Gameplay role:

- paperwork antagonist;
- flat/garage pressure;
- mission blocker;
- legalistic absurdity.

Obsession:

- stamps, notices, and "procedure".

### Bartosz Gladki

Developer. Smiling, polished, and more dangerous than the local fools because he can make harm look like opportunity.

Gameplay role:

- season antagonist;
- visible pressure from early posters and survey markers;
- finale target.

Obsession:

- selling displacement as premium lifestyle.

## Story Systems

### Przypal

Przypal is the estate noticing the player.

Narrative use:

- pressure meter;
- social consequence;
- reaction routing;
- mission variant hook later.

### World Memory

World Memory is how the story survives outside cutscenes.

Early memory:

- recent event ledger;
- reaction lines;
- HUD reasons.

Later memory:

- shop ban;
- changed patrol hint;
- altered parking state;
- visible prop/scar;
- different mission dialogue.

### Episode Missions

Each mission should work like an episode but feed the season.

Formula:

```txt
local ask
-> estate reason
-> simple objective
-> human/institutional stupidity
-> gameplay escalation
-> punchline
-> memory/consequence
```

## Season 1 Theme

Question:

```txt
Can a place that looks like a joke still be worth defending?
```

Player arc:

```txt
I came back for paperwork.
-> I need money.
-> Everyone is using me.
-> This place is broken.
-> This place is mine too.
-> I choose what kind of problem I become.
```

## Writing Rules

- Use short subtitle lines.
- Put story inside errands, signs, prompts, objects, and consequences.
- Make every mission teach one location or system.
- Let jokes have mechanical fallout.
- Let serious stakes enter through stupid objects.
- Do not copy external IP names, scenes, or catchphrases.

## First Production Target

The current playable target is the intro slice, then the first five missions. Do not expand the map until the intro loop is readable without developer guidance.

Current intro loop:

```txt
Bogus -> Zenon's shop -> Bogus -> Rysiek's gruz -> ShopTrouble -> chase -> parking
```

After that loop passes manual playtest, write and implement the first five missions before expanding scope:

1. Powrot na Rewir
2. Paragon Grozy
3. Gruzolot
4. Wersalka z Czwartego
5. Kto Zajal Miejsce?

These must prove:

- player movement/camera can carry dialogue and errands;
- shop/parking/garage have story jobs;
- Przypal and World Memory can make consequences readable;
- the developer/spoldzielnia threat is visible from the start;
- the tone is bitterly funny, not only random.
