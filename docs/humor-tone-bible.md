# Humor And Tone Bible

## North Star

The game should feel like controlled osiedle chaos with a bitter social edge:

```txt
ugly, fast, dumb, reactive, local, playable, and a little too true
```

Animation and dialogue may be stupid. Input must not be stupid.

The approved canon is **Blok 13: Rewir**: original IP, one estate, Rockstar-style consequence structure, and humor that cuts through local social absurdity.

## Tone Position

The game is not pure gag comedy. It should be funny, but the best jokes should reveal pressure underneath:

- people fight over parking because space is power;
- a receipt matters because everyone is scared of paperwork;
- a shop ban matters because the shop is the estate's economy;
- a garage matters because it is freedom, storage, and status;
- a developer poster matters because it threatens the whole rewir.

Short version:

```txt
Rockstar structure asks: why does this matter?
Walaszek bite asks: how stupid can people get about it?
```

## Tone Rules

- Make the premise local before it gets absurd.
- Give each joke a social or character reason.
- Prefer one strong joke with a gameplay consequence over five throwaway lines.
- Let NPCs overreact to small events.
- Keep dialogue short enough to read while playing.
- Use profanity as seasoning, not structure.
- Make consequences visible: HUD, camera tension, NPC behavior, shop prompts, chase pressure, later gossip.
- Let the world remember recent stupidity.
- Do not resolve everything as a joke; leave small scars in the world when useful.
- Every recurring NPC should want something, even if that desire is petty or stupid.

## Mission Satire Formula

```txt
local problem
-> personal or estate-level reason it matters
-> simple task
-> broken institution / petty ego gets in the way
-> wrong person notices
-> Przypal rises
-> escape/delivery/fix/lie
-> punchline
-> someone remembers or something changes
```

Examples:

- recover a TV remote that became a fake authority object;
- move a sofa through a staircase and ruin a parked car;
- settle a parking argument by making the whole estate worse;
- retrieve a shop receipt that everyone treats like evidence;
- deliver something cheap while everyone acts like it is contraband.
- get banned from the shop and realize it affects missions, gossip, and prices.
- uncover developer paperwork in a stupid object nobody respected.

## NPC Reaction Formula

Each reaction should answer:

- What did the player do?
- Who noticed?
- Why does that person care?
- What does the player see/hear/change?
- How long before the same joke can fire again?

Good reaction:

```txt
Player hits trash near shop.
Babka z okna raises alarm.
Przypal rises.
Zul later comments that patrols are sniffing around.
Shopkeeper gives a nervous line.
```

Bad reaction:

```txt
Random loud line plays every 3 seconds with no state change.
```

## Movement Comedy

The player can look ridiculous:

- hunched jog;
- panic sprint;
- flailing arms;
- short stumble;
- shoulder bump;
- landing dip;
- quick get-up later.

The controller must remain readable:

- no frequent random tripping;
- no long unskippable control loss;
- no camera fights during gags;
- no invisible collision jokes;
- no slapstick during precision interactions unless scripted.

## Camera Comedy

Camera may react, but it must stay useful:

- slight FOV bump during panic;
- short shake on impact;
- tiny event zoom on a big stupid collision;
- chase framing that shows escape route;
- interaction framing for quick dialogue.

Avoid:

- long cinematic interruptions;
- camera spins;
- hiding the player;
- joke zooms during steering-critical moments;
- shake strong enough to confuse direction.

## Dialogue Rules

- One line should usually fit one subtitle beat.
- NPCs can interrupt, but the player should not lose control unless the interaction is explicit.
- The player should understand the objective without a tutorial paragraph.
- Reactions should reference recent events, not explain the whole world.
- Repeated lines need cooldowns and variants.
- Use short, ciosane lines for punchlines.
- Let characters reveal worldview through petty complaints.
- Avoid long lore dumps; put story into errands, signs, prompts, and consequences.

## Character Rule

Every recurring character needs one obsession:

- bench strategist: treats parking like geopolitics;
- shopkeeper: treats receipts like sacred law;
- mechanic: believes every gruz has a soul and grudges;
- window neighbor: acts as CCTV, court, and prosecutor;
- informant: archives estate shame through gossip;
- spoldzielnia boss: weaponizes paperwork;
- developer: sells destruction as premium lifestyle.

## What To Protect

- Player can always understand where W will move them.
- Camera should explain the world, not show off.
- Przypal should create pressure, not UI panic soup.
- Comedy should emerge from systems as often as from scripted text.
- Bitter satire must not slow the game into misery; the player still needs speed, chaos, and control.
